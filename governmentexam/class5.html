Government Entrance Exams After Class 5th

Learn about the opportunities available for students after Class 5th. Discover exams, eligibility, and dates to help you plan for the future.

1. Jawahar Navodaya Vidyalaya Selection Test (JNVST)

JNVST is conducted for admission to Class 6 in Jawahar Navodaya Vidyalayas (JNVs), which are government-funded schools known for providing quality education to rural students. These schools offer free education along with boarding facilities.

Eligibility Criteria:

  • Students must be studying in Class 5 during the current academic session.
  • They must be from a recognized school within the district where the JNV is located.
  • Age Limit: The student should be between 9 to 13 years of age.
  • The candidate should not have appeared for the exam earlier.

Exam Pattern:

  • Type: Objective, Multiple-Choice Questions (MCQs)
  • Duration: 2 hours
  • Subjects Covered:
    • Mental Ability Test (MAT)
    • Arithmetic Test
    • Language Test (Regional Language)

Key Dates:

  • Registration Start: September to October (varies yearly)
  • Last Date for Registration: Generally in November
  • Exam Date: Usually in April for the main session

Registration Process:

  • Online: Through the official website: navodaya.gov.in
  • Offline: Forms are also available at district education offices.

2. National Means-cum-Merit Scholarship (NMMS)

NMMS is a scholarship exam aimed at providing financial support to meritorious students from economically weaker sections, encouraging them to continue their education after class 8.

Eligibility Criteria:

  • The student must be studying in Class 5 in a recognized school.
  • Annual family income should not exceed ₹3.5 lakh.
  • They should have scored at least 55% (50% for reserved categories) in the previous year's final examination.

Exam Pattern:

  • Type: Objective, Multiple-Choice Questions (MCQs)
  • Duration: 3 hours
  • Subjects Covered:
    • Mental Ability Test (MAT)
    • Scholastic Aptitude Test (SAT) - covering subjects like Mathematics, Science, and Social Studies

Key Dates:

  • Registration Start: August-September (varies by state)
  • Last Date for Registration: October (state-specific)
  • Exam Date: Generally in November

Registration Process:

  • Online: Through the National Scholarship Portal (NSP): scholarships.gov.in
  • State-specific: Some states have state-specific online portals.

3. Sainik School Entrance Exam (AISSEE)

The All India Sainik Schools Entrance Examination (AISSEE) is conducted for admission to Class 6 in Sainik Schools, which are aimed at preparing students for entry into the National Defence Academy (NDA) and other defence academies.

Eligibility Criteria:

  • The student must be in Class 5 or its equivalent.
  • Age Limit: Between 10 to 12 years as of March 31 of the admission year.
  • Only boys were eligible until recently, but now many Sainik Schools have started accepting girls as well.

Exam Pattern:

  • Type: Objective, Multiple-Choice Questions (MCQs)
  • Duration: 2.5 hours
  • Subjects Covered:
    • Mathematics
    • General Knowledge
    • Language Ability Test (English or Regional Language)
    • Intelligence Test

Key Dates:

  • Registration Start: October
  • Last Date for Registration: End of November
  • Exam Date: Generally in January

Registration Process:

  • Online: Through the official website: aissee.nta.nic.in

4. Rashtriya Military Schools Common Entrance Test (RMS CET)

RMS CET is an entrance test for admission to Class 6 in Rashtriya Military Schools, which provide military-oriented education.

Eligibility Criteria:

  • The student should be in Class 5.
  • Age Limit: 10 to 12 years as of March 31 of the admission year.
  • Only boys were eligible until recently, but now many Sainik Schools have started accepting girls as well.

Exam Pattern:

  • Type: Objective, Multiple-Choice Questions (MCQs)
  • Duration: 2.5 hours
  • Subjects Covered:
    • Mathematics
    • General Knowledge
    • English
    • Intelligence Test

Key Dates:

  • Registration Start: October
  • Last Date for Registration: End of November
  • Exam Date: December

Registration Process:

  • Forms available: Available on the official website or respective schools
  • Online: Visit the official website: rashtriyamilitaryschools.edu.in

5. Kendriya Vidyalaya Admission (KVS Admission)

The Kendriya Vidyalayas (KVs) are a chain of central government schools under the Ministry of Education, known for their high-quality education and uniform curriculum. Admissions to Class 5 can follow a lottery-based selection, particularly for children of government employees, though seats are also open to the general public.

Eligibility Criteria:

  • For Class 5: The child should have completed Class 4 in a recognized school.
  • Age Limit: Between 9 to 11 years as of March 31 of the admission year.

Exam Pattern:

  • Type: Merit-based, Lottery system in some cases

Key Dates:

  • Registration Start: March
  • Last Date for Registration: March-April
  • Exam Dates: May-June (for older classes)

Registration Process:

  • Online: Through the official KVS website: kvsangathan.nic.in
