Entrance Exam After 12th PCM

Learn about the opportunities available for students after Class 8. Discover exams, eligibility, and dates to help you plan for the future.

Career Options after 12th Science - PCM

1. National Defence Academy (NDA) Exam

The National Defence Academy (NDA) is the premier institution that trains candidates for officer roles in the Indian Army, Navy, and Air Force. The NDA exam is conducted by the Union Public Service Commission (UPSC) twice a year and serves as the gateway to joining the Armed Forces. Selected candidates undergo rigorous training at the academy before being commissioned as officers.

Eligibility Criteria:

  • Educational Qualification:
    • Army Wing: Passed or appearing in the 12th standard from any recognized board.
    • Air Force and Naval Wings: Passed or appearing in the 12th standard with Physics and Mathematics as subjects.
  • Age Limit: 16.5 to 19.5 years.
  • Marital Status: Only unmarried male and female candidates are eligible.
  • Nationality: Indian citizens or candidates from Bhutan, Nepal, and certain categories as specified by UPSC.
  • Physical Standards: Candidates must meet strict physical and medical standards set by the Armed Forces.

Key Dates:

  • Notification Release: January (for NDA I) and May/June (for NDA II).
  • Application Window: Opens for around 3 weeks after notification release.
  • Exam Date:
    • NDA I: April.
    • NDA II: September.
  • SSB Interview: 2-3 months after the written exam.

Registration Process:

  • Visit: Go to www.upsc.gov.in.
  • Part-I Registration: Fill in personal details, educational qualifications, and preferences.
  • Part-II Registration: Pay the application fee, choose your exam center, and upload scanned documents.
  • Fee Payment:
    • Online: Debit/Credit Card or Net Banking.
    • Offline: Through an SBI Bank Challan.
  • Admit Card: Download it from the UPSC website before the exam.
Visit Official Website

2. Indian Army Recruitment Exams

The Indian Army recruits candidates for various officer and soldier roles through exams like NDA, CDS, TGC, TES, and technical entry schemes.

Eligibility Criteria:

  • Educational Qualification:
    • Officer Entry (via NDA or CDS): Requires 12th (for NDA) or Graduation (for CDS).
    • Soldier Entry: Minimum 10th or 12th pass, depending on the role.
  • Age Limit: 16.5 to 25 years depending on the entry type.
  • Marital Status: Unmarried candidates for officer roles.
  • Physical Standards: As per Army guidelines.

Key Dates:

  • NDA: Twice a year (April and September).
  • CDS: Twice a year (February and November).

Registration Process:

  • Visit: www.joinindianarmy.nic.in.
  • Register: Create a new user account.
  • Apply: Fill in the application form, upload required documents, and submit.

5. B.Arch (Bachelor of Architecture)

Overview :Bachelor of Architecture (B.Arch) is a 5-year undergraduate professional course that focuses on designing, planning, and constructing buildings, landscapes, and urban spaces. It combines creativity and technical knowledge to train future architects.

Eligibility Criteria:

  • 12th pass with Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics (PCM) as mandatory subjects.
  • Minimum 50% aggregate in PCM.
  • Candidates with a 10+3 Diploma (Mathematics as a subject) are also eligible.
  • No upper age limit, but specific requirements depend on the entrance exam.

Key Dates:

  • NATA Application: Expected in February 2024.
  • NATA Exam Dates: April 2024 (Test 1) and June 2024 (Test 2).
  • JEE Main Paper 2 Application: December 2023 (January Session).
  • JEE Main Paper 2 Exam Dates: January 2024 (Session 1) and April 2024 (Session 2).

Registration Process:

  • NATA: Register on the official portal (www.nata.in), fill in details, upload documents, and pay ₹2000 (general category).
  • JEE Main Paper 2: Register on the NTA portal (www.jeemain.nta.nic.in), complete the form, upload documents, and pay ₹1000 (general category).

Government Exams for B.Tech Admission

1. JEE Main (Joint Entrance Examination Main)

Overview: JEE Main is one of the most popular entrance exams for B.Tech admission in India, conducted by the National Testing Agency (NTA). It serves as the gateway for admission to NITs, IIITs, and other central government-funded technical institutions.

Eligibility Criteria:

  • Educational Qualification: Must have passed 12th with Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics.
  • Age Limit: Candidates should be born on or after October 1, 1999 (General category) for eligibility in JEE Main (Check specific age criteria for reserved categories).
  • Number of Attempts: Candidates can attempt JEE Main up to 3 times in consecutive years.

Key Dates:

  • Registration: December/January
  • Exam Dates: January and April (Twice a year)
  • Result Announcement: Within 1-2 weeks after the exam.

Registration Process:

  • Register: Visit the official NTA JEE website (https://jeemain.nta.nic.in/), create an account, and fill in the personal details, educational qualifications, and preferences.
  • Application Fee: Pay the application fee online (debit/credit card, net banking).
  • Document Upload: Upload scanned documents such as a photograph, signature, and valid ID proof.
  • Submit: Submit the application form and download the admit card to take the exam.
Visit Official Website

State Level Private Exams For B.Tech Admission

1. Tamil Nadu Engineering Admission (TNEA)

TNEA is the admission process conducted by Anna University, Chennai, for students who wish to pursue B.Tech and B.Arch programs in various government and private engineering colleges in Tamil Nadu. It is a state-level centralized admission process for engineering seats.

Eligibility Criteria:

  • Educational Qualification: Candidates must have passed the 12th standard (or equivalent) examination with Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics as compulsory subjects. Minimum aggregate marks: 50% (40% for SC/ST candidates) in Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics. For B.Arch: Candidates must have studied Mathematics as one of the subjects in the 12th standard.
  • Age Limit: Candidates should be born on or after July 1, 2003 (for general category), and should not exceed the maximum age limit as prescribed by the Tamil Nadu government for admissions.
  • Domicile: Tamil Nadu residents or candidates who have completed their schooling from Tamil Nadu can apply. Non-resident Indians (NRIs) can also apply if they satisfy certain criteria.

Key Dates:

  • Notification Release: May (varies every year).
  • Application Window: June - July.
  • Counseling Starts: Typically in July or August.
  • Class Commencement: Around August or September.

Registration Process:

  • Visit the Official Website: Go to TNEA Official Website.
  • Fill Application Form: Register on the website, fill in personal details, educational qualifications, and preferences for college and course.
  • Document Submission: Upload scanned copies of required documents such as mark sheets, community certificate (if applicable), etc.
  • Application Fee: Pay the application fee online via net banking, credit/debit card, or through an offline challan.
  • Seat Allotment: Based on marks obtained in 12th standard, candidates are assigned a rank, and seat allotment is done through counseling.
  • Counseling: Attend the counseling session, during which candidates can select their preferred college and branch based on their rank.
Visit Official Website

2. Bihar Combined Entrance Competitive Examination (BCECE)

BCECE is a state-level entrance exam conducted by the BCECE Board for admission to engineering, medical, and agricultural courses in various government and private colleges in Bihar. For engineering aspirants, BCECE is the key exam to secure admission to B.Tech programs in Bihar.

Eligibility Criteria:

  • Educational Qualification: Candidates must have passed or be appearing in the 12th standard (or equivalent) with Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics as compulsory subjects. Minimum aggregate marks required: 45% for general category (40% for SC/ST).
  • Age Limit: Candidates must be at least 17 years of age as of December 31 of the exam year. There is no upper age limit for B.Tech candidates.
  • Domicile: Candidates must be Indian citizens. Preference is given to candidates who have completed their schooling in Bihar.

Key Dates:

  • Notification Release: March (varies every year).
  • Application Window: March - April.
  • BCECE Exam Date: Typically held in May.
  • Result Declaration: June.
  • Counseling: Begins after the declaration of results.

Registration Process:

  • Visit the Official Website: Go to BCECE Official Website.
  • Fill Application Form: Register with personal details and educational qualifications.
  • Upload Documents: Upload scanned copies of necessary documents such as 12th mark sheet, photograph, and signature.
  • Application Fee: Pay the application fee via online banking, debit/credit cards, or through a bank challan.
  • Admit Card: Download the admit card from the website.
  • Exam: Appear for the BCECE exam. The exam is usually a 2-stage test (Stage 1 and Stage 2).
  • Result and Counseling: After the result, candidates who qualify are eligible for counseling, where seats are allotted based on the rank.
