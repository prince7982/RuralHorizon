Entrance Exam After 12th Arts

Learn about the opportunities available for students after Class 8. Discover exams, eligibility, and dates to help you plan for the future.

Government Exams for Admission to Arts & Humanities Courses

1. DUET (Delhi University Entrance Test)

Overview: The Delhi University Entrance Test (DUET) is conducted by Delhi University for admission to various undergraduate and postgraduate programs, especially in arts, humanities, and social sciences. The test assesses candidates' aptitude in relevant subjects and streamlines the selection process for competitive courses offered by the university.

Eligibility Criteria:

  • For undergraduate programs: A 12th pass in any stream (Arts, Commerce, Science).
  • For postgraduate programs: A bachelor's degree in the relevant field.

Key Dates:

  • Application period: May to June (typically).
  • Exam date: Usually held in June or July.

Registration Process:

  • Visit the official DUET portal (du.ac.in).
  • Create a profile by registering with personal and academic details.
  • Fill out the application form and upload required documents.
  • Pay the registration fee (online).
  • Submit the form and download the acknowledgment slip.

Government Law Entrance Exams

1. Common Law Admission Test (CLAT)

Overview:The Common Law Admission Test (CLAT) is a national-level entrance exam conducted for admission to undergraduate and postgraduate law courses at 22 National Law Universities (NLUs) in India. It is the most popular law entrance exam for students aspiring to pursue law at the top law schools in India.

Eligibility Criteria:

  • Undergraduate (UG): 12th pass or equivalent with at least 45% marks (40% for SC/ST candidates).
  • Postgraduate (PG): A law degree (LL.B) from a recognized university with a minimum of 50% marks (45% for SC/ST candidates).

Key Dates:

  • Application Window: January to April (usually).
  • Exam Date: May (check the official website for exact dates).

Registration Process:

  • Visit the official website: https://consortiumofnlus.ac.in.
  • Register for the exam by filling in your personal details and selecting the exam center.
  • Fill out the application form, upload documents (photo, signature), and pay the application fee.
  • Submit the form and take a printout for future reference.
Visit Official Website

Other Law Entrance Exams

1. Maharashtra State Law CET

Overview:The Maharashtra State Law Common Entrance Test (Maharashtra State Law CET) is a state-level examination conducted for admission to various law courses, including LLB programs, offered by government and private law colleges in Maharashtra. This exam is a crucial step for students seeking to pursue a law degree in the state.

Eligibility Criteria:

  • 12th pass (or equivalent) from a recognized board.
  • Minimum marks: 45% for general category, 40% for reserved categories.
  • Age limit: Generally not specified, but must meet educational qualification requirements.

Key Dates:

  • Notification Release: March (varies each year).
  • Application Window: April - May.
  • Exam Date: Typically held in June.
  • Result Declaration: July.
  • Counseling: Starts after results are declared, usually in July or August.

Registration Process:

  • Visit the Official Website: Go to the Maharashtra CET website.
  • Fill the Application Form: Register with personal details, academic qualifications, and select the preferred exam center.
  • Document Upload: Upload scanned copies of necessary documents like the 12th mark sheet, photo, signature, etc.
  • Application Fee Payment: Pay the application fee via net banking, credit/debit card, or bank challan.
  • Admit Card: Download the admit card once released.
  • Appear for the Exam: Take the Maharashtra State Law CET on the scheduled date.
  • Result and Counseling: After the exam, results will be declared, and the counseling process will begin for seat allotment.
Visit Official Website

2. Delhi University Entrance Test (DUET)

Overview:The Delhi University Entrance Test (DUET) is conducted by the University of Delhi for admission to various undergraduate, postgraduate, and M.Phil/Ph.D. programs, including law courses. The DUET exam is mandatory for admission to law programs such as LLB at the University of Delhi.

Eligibility Criteria:

Key Dates:

  • Notification Release: Usually in May (varies each year).
  • Application Window: May - June.
  • Exam Date: Typically held in June or July.
  • Result Declaration: July.
  • Counseling: Starts after the result declaration, usually in July or August.

Registration Process:

  • Visit the Official Website: Go to the DUET official website.
  • Fill the Application Form: Complete the online registration by filling in personal details, academic qualifications, and choosing the exam center.
  • Document Upload: Upload scanned copies of documents like 12th mark sheet, photo, and signature.
  • Application Fee Payment: Pay the application fee online using net banking, credit/debit card, or other available payment methods.
  • Admit Card: Download the admit card once released.
  • Exam: Appear for the DUET exam on the scheduled date.
  • Result and Counseling: Results will be declared, and counseling for seat allocation will follow.
