Government Entrance Exams After Class 8

Learn about the opportunities available for students after Class 8. Discover exams, eligibility, and dates to help you plan for the future.

1. Jawahar Navodaya Vidyalaya Selection Test (JNVST)

JNVST is conducted for admission into Class 9 at Jawahar Navodaya Vidyalayas (JNVs), offering quality education and boarding facilities for students from rural areas.

Eligibility:

  • Currently studying in Class 8 at a recognized school within the district of the JNV.
  • Age: Between 13 to 15 years.
  • No prior attempt for this exam.

Exam Pattern:

  • Objective MCQs
  • 2.5 hours duration
  • Subjects: Mental Ability, Arithmetic, and Language Test

Key Dates: Registrations open from September; the exam is usually conducted in February.

Visit Official Website

2. National Means-cum-Merit Scholarship (NMMS)

NMMS is a scholarship exam aimed at providing financial support to meritorious students from economically weaker sections, encouraging them to continue their education after class 8.

Eligibility Criteria:

  • The student must be studying in Class 8 in a recognized school.
  • Annual family income should not exceed ₹3.5 lakh.
  • They should have scored at least 55% (50% for reserved categories) in the previous year's final examination.

Exam Pattern:

  • Type: Objective, Multiple-Choice Questions (MCQs)
  • Duration: 3 hours
  • Subjects Covered: Mental Ability Test (MAT), Scholastic Aptitude Test (SAT) - covering subjects like Mathematics, Science, and Social Studies

Key Dates:

  • Registration Start: August-September (varies by state)
  • Last Date for Registration: October (state-specific)
  • Exam Date: Generally in November

Registration Process:

  • Through the National Scholarship Portal (NSP): scholarships.gov.in
  • Some states have state-specific online portals.
Visit Official Website

3. Sainik School Entrance Exam (AISSEE)

The All India Sainik Schools Entrance Examination (AISSEE) is conducted for admission to Class 9 in Sainik Schools, which are aimed at preparing students for entry into the National Defence Academy (NDA) and other defence academies.

Eligibility Criteria:

  • The student must be in Class 8 or its equivalent.
  • Age Limit: Between 13 to 15 years as of March 31 of the admission year.
  • Sainik Schools accept both boys and girls.

Exam Pattern:

  • Type: Objective, Multiple-Choice Questions (MCQs)
  • Duration: 3 hours
  • Subjects Covered:
    • Mathematics
    • General Knowledge
    • Language Ability Test (English or Regional Language)
    • Intelligence Test

Key Dates:

  • Registration Start: October
  • Last Date for Registration: End of November
  • Exam Date: Generally in January

Registration Process:

  • Online through the official website: aissee.nta.nic.in
Visit Official Website

5. Kendriya Vidyalaya Admission (KVS Admission)

The Kendriya Vidyalayas (KVs) are a chain of central government schools under the Ministry of Education, known for their high-quality education and uniform curriculum. Admissions to Class 9 can follow a lottery-based selection, particularly for children of government employees, though seats are also open to the general public if availability permits.

Eligibility Criteria:

  • The student should be currently in Class 8.
  • Priority is given to children of government employees, but all students can apply based on seat availability.

Key Features:

  • No entrance exam.
  • Merit-based selection.
  • Special quotas for various categories.

Key Dates:

  • Registration Start: April
  • Last Date for Registration: May
  • Merit List Announcement: June

Registration Process:

  • Online through the official KVS website: kvsonlineadmission.kvs.gov.in

6. Central Hindu School (CHS) Entrance Exam for Class 9

The Central Hindu School (CHS) is affiliated with the Banaras Hindu University (BHU) and is one of the most reputed schools in India. The CHS Entrance Exam is conducted for admission to Class 9 and Class 11. It focuses on selecting students who show academic excellence and promise.

Eligibility Criteria:

  • Candidates must have passed Class 8 from a recognized school.
  • The age of the candidate should be between 13 and 15 years as of September 30 of the admission year.

Exam Pattern:

  • The exam is conducted in a written format, with objective-type questions.
  • Subjects Covered: Mathematics, General Science, Social Science, English, Hindi.
  • Duration: 2 hours.
  • Total marks usually range from 100 to 150, depending on the year's guidelines.

Key Dates:

  • Application Start: Generally in February.
  • Last Date for Registration: March.
  • Exam Date: Typically in April or May.
  • Result Declaration: May or June.

Registration Process:

  • Visit the BHU official website for school admissions: bhuonline.in.
  • Register with the candidate’s basic details.
  • Fill in the application form, providing academic details and selecting the exam center.
  • Upload documents like photographs and signatures.
  • Pay the application fee and submit the form.
  • Download the admit card for the examination.
Visit Official Website

7. Rashtriya Indian Military College (RIMC) Entrance Exam for Class 9

The Rashtriya Indian Military College (RIMC) in Dehradun is a premier institution that prepares young boys for the National Defence Academy (NDA). The RIMC Entrance Exam is held twice a year for admission to Class 9.

Eligibility Criteria:

  • Candidates should be studying in Class 8 or have passed Class 8 from a recognized school.
  • Only boys are eligible.
  • The age should be between 13 and 14 years as of January 1 or July 1 of the admission year.

Exam Pattern:

  • The entrance exam has three parts: English (125 marks), Mathematics (200 marks), and General Knowledge (75 marks).
  • The exam is followed by a Viva-Voce (interview) of 50 marks.
  • Duration for the English and General Knowledge papers is 1.5 hours, while the Mathematics paper is 2.5 hours.

Key Dates:

  • Application Start: February/March or August/September (depending on the cycle).
  • Last Date for Registration: April or October.
  • Exam Date: June or December.
  • Result Declaration: After the interview, usually within a month.

Registration Process:

  • Obtain the application form from RIMC's official website: rimc.gov.in.
  • Fill in the application form, attach a Demand Draft (DD) as payment, and submit it to the respective state government education department.
  • Appear for the written test and Viva-Voce for selection.
Visit Official Website

8. National Talent Search Examination (NTSE)

NTSE is a national-level scholarship program for Class 8 students that identifies talented students across the country. Although NTSE is not a direct admission exam, qualifying in NTSE can provide scholarships for higher studies and is a prestigious recognition.

Eligibility Criteria:

  • Candidates should be in Class 8 from a recognized school.
  • Open to all Indian students.

Exam Pattern:

  • NTSE is conducted in two stages:
  • Stage I (State Level) - Mental Ability Test (MAT) and Scholastic Aptitude Test (SAT).
  • Stage II (National Level) - Same pattern as Stage I.
  • Each paper is for 100 marks, with a total of 200 marks for both stages.
  • Duration: 2 hours per paper.

Key Dates:

  • Stage I Application Start: August or September.
  • Stage I Exam Date: November.
  • Stage II Exam Date: May or June (for qualified students).
  • Result Declaration: July (Stage II).

Registration Process:

  • Visit the respective State/UT Education Department’s website or NCERT’s official website: ncert.nic.in.
  • Download the application form or apply online (based on the state's guidelines).
  • Fill in the form and submit it to the respective authority.
  • Appear for Stage I, and if qualified, proceed to Stage II.
Visit Official Website

  • Application Form: Fill out the online application form for Class 9 admission, including personal and academic information.
  • Upload Documents: Upload necessary documents such as proof of residence, school certificates, etc.
  • Fee Payment: Pay the application fee online, if applicable.
  • Download Admit Card: After successful registration, download the admit card for the exam.

Exam Pattern:

  • Duration: 2 hours.
  • Subjects: Mathematics, Science, Social Science, English, and General Knowledge.
  • Types of Questions: Objective-type questions (MCQs).
